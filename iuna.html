Musical Instruments

Iúna

Iúna is believed to have originated in the state of Bahia, Brazil, and is named after a river in the region. It is considered one of the oldest and most traditional rhythms in capoeira, and is often associated with the Angola style of capoeira.

In addition to its use in the roda (capoeira circle), Iúna is also used in capoeira music, with songs often referencing the rhythm's slow and meditative qualities. Some well-known capoeira songs that use Iúna include "Iúna" by Mestre Bimba and "Iúna da Praia" by Mestre Pastinha.

In the roda, Iúna is often used to set the tone for the game, with players using its slow tempo to showcase their control and technique. It is also commonly used in the Angola style of capoeira, which emphasizes fluidity and improvisation over acrobatics and flashy moves.
